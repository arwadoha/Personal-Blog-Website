The Fascinating Life of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are among the most vibrant and diverse ecosystems on Earth, often referred to as the "rainforests of the sea." These underwater structures, built by tiny coral polyps, are home to countless species of marine life, providing shelter, food, and breeding grounds. Beyond their beauty, coral reefs play a crucial role in maintaining ocean health, protecting coastlines from erosion, and supporting the livelihoods of millions of people worldwide. However, they face growing threats from climate change, pollution, and overfishing, making it more important than ever to understand and protect these natural wonders.
